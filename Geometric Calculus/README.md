## Summary

Wrap a string around a coffee cup, measure the circumference, measure the diameter, divide: the ratio is 3.14159…, stable across the cup's size, the string's material, the country, and the century. The ratio is a property of how space closes on itself, and from that single measurement every constant the paper uses is determined without additional empirical input.

The accumulation ratio e emerges because constant curvature is Markovian — each step depends only on the current position — and e is the unique function whose rate of change equals its current value. The perpendicular coupling i emerges because the plane has exactly one way to divide itself into the minimum number of self-sufficient equal pieces, which forces 90° and i² = −1 by the dimensionality of the space rather than by convention. Euler's identity e^{iπ} + 1 = 0 then reads as three measurements of one circle composed: the equation is a law in the same sense F = ma is a law, and changing it means changing what a string returns when wrapped around a circle.

Zooming in on a point of a circle or sphere reveals another circle or sphere — the geometry is self-similar. The local object is a closed curve rather than an open line, and the chain rule for closed curves acquires a correction term that flat calculus sets to zero. The four rules built around that correction are σ-calculus, and several results of modern physics emerge from them as special cases rather than independent discoveries:

- Schrödinger's equation comes from the σ-derivative, with the Hamiltonian generating the rate of change and the time-evolution operator accumulating it through the σ-integral.
- Friston's Free Energy Principle comes from the same σ-integral applied to compact geometry.
- The Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula that quantum computing uses to compile gate sequences comes from the σ-chain rule written explicitly.
- The Born rule of quantum mechanics comes from the projection theorem rather than from a postulate.
- Newton's calculus is the zero-curvature limit of all four rules, recovered when closed circles flatten into lines and the correction term vanishes.

Euclid's fifth postulate — that two non-intersecting lines never meet — follows the same path. The first four postulates are derivable from the three properties of the exponential path: defined everywhere, continuous, and periodic. The fifth postulate asserts that two things can coexist in physical space with zero interaction, but the periodicity of every exponential path means every path returns to identity, which means every path interacts with every other path. Parallelism is what the fifth postulate looks like when the return point recedes beyond the observer's horizon.

## Role in the Series

The Geometrical Theory of Communication derives what the boundary operation computes; this paper derives the four rules for computing it on curved space. The Shape of Reality uses σ-calculus as its mathematical spine for deriving physical constants, and every proof in the particle mass and coupling constant sections depends on the derivative and integral rules established here.
