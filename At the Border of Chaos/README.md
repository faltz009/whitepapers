## Summary
This paper introduces the core thesis of the series: information becomes usable when continuous reality is discretized into boundaries. Naming, categorizing, and measurement all perform this boundary operation.  

From that starting point, the paper argues that:
- paradoxes (Theseus, Achilles, self-reference) are boundary effects,
- observer-dependent projection creates complementary descriptions,
- entropy and causality emerge as dual outcomes of discretization,
- limits in language, science, and formal systems are structural, not accidental.

## Role in the Series
This is the conceptual foundation paper.  
Later papers formalize the operations (`Ax = b` framing), propose architectural constraints for self-referential systems, and extend the framework into dynamics and physical instantiation.
