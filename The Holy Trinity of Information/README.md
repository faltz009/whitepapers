## Summary
This paper proposes a triadic architecture for stable self-referential systems.  
The main claim is that identity maintenance under entropy pressure requires three interacting roles at different timescales, rather than a single undifferentiated loop.

Core points:
- self-reference is treated as a productive process, not just paradox,
- identity is maintained through recursive verification dynamics,
- architectural separation prevents collapse in self-modifying systems,
- similar constraints appear across physical, biological, and cognitive scales.

## Role in the Series
This is the architectural layer of the framework.  
It extends the boundary and operational claims of the first two papers into a concrete organization principle for adaptive systems.
