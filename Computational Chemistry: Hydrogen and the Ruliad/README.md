## Summary

The abstract claims of the framework need a concrete physical system where anyone can verify the correspondence directly. Hydrogen provides that system, because it is the minimal atom: one proton, one electron, one binding interaction — the same three roles as S1/S2/S3, running at femtosecond timescales through the electromagnetic force.

The paper distinguishes abstract bits, which measure capacity, from physically instantiated verification structures, which carry the algebraic relationships that make meaning possible, and shows that hydrogen models as a minimal triadic verification unit. The rotational and covering-space geometry of hydrogen's orbital structure maps onto the hyperspherical S³ interpretation precisely, connecting orbital language to the quaternion carrier language of the framework without forcing the identification. Iterative hydrogen interactions then provide a concrete path into Wolfram's computational-universe reasoning: if the Ruliad's local geometry is S³ and elementary interactions are Hamilton products, then chemistry is the lowest level at which that geometry becomes directly measurable.

## Role in the Series

The architectural papers establish what the verification structure must look like; this paper shows it already exists at atomic scale.
