## Summary

Working independently in Brazil, the author arrived at the same core structures that Stepney, Levin, and collaborators reached from the direction of biological computation and open-ended evolution: a dual account of time in life, self-reference reframed as a productive and time-extended operation, constraint-first geometry for navigating vast possibility spaces without brute-force search, and hierarchical architectures for self-modifying open systems.

The commentary documents that convergence precisely. On time: living systems construct a representational time through compressed sequences and modeled futures that is causally distinct from physical time, and the S1/S2/S3 architecture implements this split directly — S1 lives in the physical present, S3 carries the compressed past, and S2 mediates between them at the moment of choice. On self-reference: the A?=A operation generates information through the gap between expectation and outcome, which is exactly the productive self-reference that Stepney et al. identify as enabling rather than paradoxical. On constraint: the S1-to-S3 prohibition is the constraint that rules out hallucination and makes the vast design space navigable without exhaustive search. On Levin specifically: bioelectric morphogenesis — voltage-mediated pattern maintenance across cell replacement, gap-junction collective fields, ion-channel body-plan memory — is S1/S2/S3 at cellular scale with substrate changed but architecture preserved.
