## Summary
This paper operationalizes the boundary framework introduced in *At the Border of Chaos*. It focuses on how syntactic information becomes semantic and effective information.

Core claims:
- Shannon Level A (transmission) is insufficient for meaning,
- meaning emerges at the structured boundary between order and noise,
- interaction is the irreducible primitive (`to exist is to interact`),
- the transformation form `Ax = b` captures a universal computational relation,
- differentiation and integration act as complementary operations that generate usable meaning.

## Role in the Series
This is the formal/operational bridge paper.  
It connects philosophical framing to implementable structure, preparing the architectural and dynamic extensions developed in later papers.
